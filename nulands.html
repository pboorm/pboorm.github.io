X-ray models

For NuLANDS we are fitting a large library of X-ray spectral models to every source with BXA, which combines the nested sampling algorithm with astronomical X-ray spectral fitting packages such as PyXspec and Sherpa.. This enables us to account for a wide range of different physical phenomena in a computationally-efficient manner. We split our X-ray spectral models into four 'classes', which include B(asic), U(nobscured), O(bscured) and P(hysical) models. Source catalogue

This section lists the full catalogue of warm IRAS sources within a redshift of 0.044 with their corresponding optical classifications. The columns for each table are as follows:
  • ID: The ID number from de Grijp et al., (1987).
  • NED: The source name from NED with associated URL.
  • Simbad: The source name from Simbad with associated URL.
  • Swift/BAT: The 105-month Swift/BAT survey ID with URL to the corresponding source page.
  • R. A.: J2000 source right ascension in degrees.
  • Dec.: J2000 source declination in degrees.
  • Type: The optical spectroscopic type of the source, using the Veron-Cetty & Veron (2010) classifications.
  • z: The spectroscopic redshift of the source.
  • Dist.: Distance to the source in Mpc. Entries marked with * correspond to the median redshift-independent distance from NED-D.
